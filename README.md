## Business Problem
Residential property portfolios often contain hidden revenue opportunities caused by below-market rents, long-tenure leases, and operational inefficiencies. At the same time, property managers must monitor tenant arrears, maintenance costs, and portfolio performance while while supporting operational and investment decision-making.

## Business Question

This project was developed to provide answers to 3 key business themes:

**Revenue Optimization**
- How much revenue is currently generated by the portfolio?
- Where is the greatest revenue optimization opportunity? 
- Which units offer the highest revenue upside through rent increases or renovation?
- How efficiently is the portfolio capturing rent relative to legal benchmark levels?

**Collection Risk**
- Which tenants represent the highest collection risk? 

**Maintenance Performance**
- What financial impact do maintenance costs have on portfolio performance?

## Business Impact
The analytical solution generated several actionable business insights:
Portfolio Performance
•	Confirmed a strong occupancy rate of 98.85%. 
Revenue Optimization
•	Identified over €309K in embedded annual revenue upside. 
•	Quantified €407K in theoretical annual revenue opportunity from legal rent gaps. 
•	Identified €79K in unrealized annual rental revenue opportunities. 
•	Prioritized long-tenure units representing approximately €207K in additional revenue potential. 
Credit Risk
•	Measured revenue at risk at 6.07% of annual rental revenue. 
•	Revealed that 94% of outstanding receivables originate from regulated units. 
•	Identified a significant concentration risk, with one tenant (BLDG_01 Apt # -2J ) accounting for 30% of total outstanding receivables. 
Operational Performance
•	Confirmed a low maintenance burden of 1.01% of annual rental revenue. 

## Business Recommendations 
•	Review 13 low-rent units (<€1,000) for potential rent adjustments. 
•	Prioritize long-tenure units (>10 years) for phased rent optimization. 
•	Strengthen collection strategies for high-risk tenants.
These findings provide management with clear, data-driven insights to improve revenue performance, prioritize collection efforts, optimize maintenance planning, and support investment decisions.

## Challenges Solved
 
- Inconsistent country naming across datasets.
- Missing values in developing-country records.
- Different reporting years across sources.
- Unit standardization and data harmonization.
